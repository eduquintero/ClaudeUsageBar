How to Set Up Claude Usage Notifications on Mac

Wouldn't it be great to get a notification before you hit your Claude limit? Here's how to set up smart usage alerts on your Mac so you never get cut off mid-conversation again.

The Problem: No Built-In Usage Notifications

If you use Claude regularly, you already know the frustration. You are deep into a conversation, refining a complex prompt, or working through a multi-step coding task, and suddenly Claude tells you that you have hit your usage limit. No warning, no heads up. Just a hard stop.

This is especially painful for Claude Code users. During agentic coding sessions, Claude Code can burn through your usage limit rapidly as it reads files, writes code, runs commands, and iterates on solutions. One moment everything is flowing, and the next you are locked out with no way to finish what you started.

Anthropic does not provide a built-in Claude usage notification system. There is no setting on claude.ai that says "notify me when I reach 75% of my limit." You are expected to manually check your usage page, which nobody actually does in the middle of a productive session.

The Solution: ClaudeUsageBar Smart Notifications

ClaudeUsageBar is a free, open-source macOS menu bar app that solves this problem with a 4-tier Claude usage alert system. It monitors your usage in the background and sends you native macOS notifications at key thresholds before you hit your limit.

4-Tier Notification System

25%
Heads Up
A gentle reminder that you have started using your allowance. Plenty of capacity remaining.
50%
Halfway There
You have used half your limit. Good time to prioritize your remaining tasks.
75%
Getting Close
Three-quarters consumed. Start wrapping up non-essential conversations.
90%
Almost at Limit
Critical threshold. Save your work and finish your most important task now.

Each notification uses native macOS notifications, so they look and feel like any other system alert. They appear in your Notification Center and follow your existing notification preferences.

Setting Up Notifications in 4 Steps

Getting Claude limit notifications working on your Mac takes less than two minutes. Here is how to do it.

1

Download and install ClaudeUsageBar

Grab the latest version from the official download page. Open the DMG file and drag ClaudeUsageBar into your Applications folder. Launch it, and you will see the icon appear in your menu bar.

2

Get your session cookie from claude.ai

Go to claude.ai and open Developer Tools with Cmd+Option+I. Navigate to the Network tab, refresh the page, and click any request. Find the Cookie header in the Request Headers section and copy its full value.

3

Paste it into ClaudeUsageBar

Click the ClaudeUsageBar icon in your menu bar and select "Set Session Cookie." Paste the cookie value you copied and confirm. The app will immediately fetch your current usage data.

4

Notifications are enabled automatically

That's it. There is no extra configuration needed. ClaudeUsageBar will now monitor your usage and send you Claude rate limit warnings at each threshold. macOS may ask you to allow notifications from ClaudeUsageBar the first time -- make sure to click Allow.

Tip: If you do not see notifications, go to System Settings > Notifications > ClaudeUsageBar and make sure notifications are enabled. Check that "Allow Notifications" is toggled on and your preferred alert style is selected.

How the Notification System Works

Understanding how the Claude code notification system works behind the scenes can help you get the most out of it. Here is what happens under the hood.

ClaudeUsageBar checks your usage data by querying the same API endpoint that the claude.ai usage page uses. It does this automatically every 5 minutes, so you always have near-real-time information without hammering the API.

When your usage crosses one of the four thresholds (25%, 50%, 75%, or 90%), the app fires a native macOS notification through the system Notification Center. This means notifications work the way you expect them to:

  • Appear even when the app is in the background. You do not need to have ClaudeUsageBar focused or visible. As long as it is running in the menu bar, notifications will fire.
  • Respect Do Not Disturb settings. If you have Focus mode or Do Not Disturb enabled, ClaudeUsageBar notifications follow those rules like any other app.
  • Do not repeat for the same threshold. If you have already been notified at 75%, you will not get another 75% notification until your usage resets. No notification spam.
  • Show in Notification Center history. Even if you miss a banner notification, you can find it in your Notification Center by swiping from the right edge of your trackpad.

Customizing Your Notification Experience

Beyond the notification system itself, ClaudeUsageBar gives you several ways to keep tabs on your usage at a glance.

Menu bar color coding. The usage percentage displayed in your menu bar changes color based on your current usage level. Green means you are in good shape, yellow means you are approaching your limit, and red means you are critically close. This gives you a passive, always-visible indicator without needing any notifications at all.

Quick check with Cmd+U. Press Cmd+U from anywhere on your Mac to instantly open the ClaudeUsageBar popup. This shows your current session usage, weekly usage (for Pro users), and the exact time when your usage resets. It is the fastest way to do a manual check when you want more detail than the menu bar percentage provides.

Auto-refresh every 5 minutes. ClaudeUsageBar refreshes your usage data automatically. You never need to manually refresh, though you can click the refresh button in the popup if you want an instant update. The 5-minute interval keeps you informed without creating unnecessary API requests.

Why Notifications Matter for Claude Code Users

If you use Claude Code, usage notifications are not just a convenience. They are essential to your workflow.

Claude Code operates autonomously during agentic coding sessions. It reads your codebase, writes implementations, runs tests, debugs errors, and iterates -- all in rapid succession. A single Claude Code session can easily consume 30-50% of your usage allowance in under an hour, especially when working on complex refactoring tasks or implementing new features across multiple files.

Without a Claude code notification system, you might start a major refactoring session at 60% usage and hit your limit halfway through, leaving your codebase in a partially modified state. With ClaudeUsageBar's notifications, you get a warning at 75% and a critical alert at 90%, giving you time to:

  • Save your current progress by committing working changes before the limit hits
  • Prioritize remaining tasks so the most important changes get completed first
  • Plan around reset times by checking when your usage window resets and scheduling heavy sessions accordingly
  • Avoid wasted context by wrapping up conversations cleanly instead of getting cut off mid-thought

This is why many developers consider ClaudeUsageBar essential for their Claude Code workflow. The few minutes it takes to set up can save you hours of frustration from unexpected rate limits.
