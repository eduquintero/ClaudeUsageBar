Claude Code Usage Tracker: Monitor Your Limits in Real Time

Claude Code has become the go-to AI coding assistant for developers who want to move fast. Whether you are scaffolding a new project, debugging production issues, or refactoring a legacy codebase, Claude Code sits in your terminal and delivers results that feel like pair programming with a senior engineer. But there is a catch that every heavy user runs into sooner or later: usage limits.

Anthropic enforces rate limits on Claude Code to keep the service stable and fair. The problem is that there is no native, always-visible indicator telling you how close you are to hitting that wall. One moment you are deep in a flow state, iterating on a complex feature. The next moment, Claude Code tells you to wait. Your momentum is gone, and you have no idea when you will get capacity back.

That is exactly why ClaudeUsageBar exists -- a free, open-source Claude Code usage tracker that lives in your macOS menu bar and gives you real-time visibility into your remaining capacity.

Why You Need a Claude Code Usage Tracker

If you rely on Claude Code daily, you already know the pain. You are mid-session, working through a tricky implementation, sending prompt after prompt. Claude Code is generating tests, fixing type errors, and wiring up API routes. Then it stops. Rate limited. No warning, no countdown, no visibility into what happened.

The core issue is zero native visibility. Claude Code does not surface your usage percentage in the terminal. There is no progress bar, no color-coded indicator, nothing that tells you "you have used 85% of your session allowance, consider pacing yourself." You are flying blind until you slam into the wall.

This creates real problems for developers:

  • Lost flow state. Context-switching out of deep work because of an unexpected rate limit is one of the most expensive interruptions a developer can face. Studies consistently show it takes 15-25 minutes to get back into deep focus.
  • Poor planning. Without knowing your remaining capacity, you cannot prioritize which tasks to tackle with Claude Code versus which to handle manually. You end up wasting usage on low-value prompts.
  • Session management blindness. Claude Code usage resets on a rolling window, but without tracking the reset time, you do not know whether to wait 10 minutes or 3 hours.
  • No proactive alerts. By the time you learn you have hit the limit, it is too late. A good notification system would warn you at 75% or 90% so you can adjust.

A dedicated Claude Code usage tracker solves all of this. Instead of being reactive, you become proactive about your AI-assisted development workflow.

How ClaudeUsageBar Tracks Your Claude Code Usage

ClaudeUsageBar is a lightweight macOS menu bar app that fetches your Claude usage data and displays it as a clean percentage right in your menu bar. Here is how it works under the hood and what you see as a user.

Always-visible percentage

The app shows your current usage percentage directly in the macOS menu bar, next to your other system icons. At a glance, you know exactly where you stand. No need to open a browser, navigate to a settings page, or run a command. The number is just there, always.

Color-coded status indicators

ClaudeUsageBar uses an intuitive color system so you can assess your usage status without even reading the number:

  • Green -- You are under 70% usage. Plenty of room to keep prompting.
  • Yellow -- You are between 70% and 90%. Start being more intentional with your prompts.
  • Red -- You are above 90%. You are about to hit the limit. Wrap up or wait for the reset.

Session and weekly tracking

Click the menu bar icon to expand the detail panel. You will see two progress bars: one for your session limit (the rolling window that resets every few hours) and one for your weekly limit. Both show exact percentages, color-coded indicators, and the precise reset time. This dual view lets you plan not just your current session but your entire week. For a deeper breakdown of how these limits differ, see our guide on Claude Code vs Claude Pro usage.

Auto-refresh every 5 minutes

The usage data refreshes automatically every 5 minutes, so the number in your menu bar is always current. You do not need to manually check or click anything. The app quietly polls in the background and updates the display. If you want an immediate refresh, just click the "Refresh" button in the detail panel.

Global keyboard shortcut

Press Cmd+U from anywhere on your Mac to instantly open the ClaudeUsageBar detail panel. You do not need to move your mouse to the menu bar or switch windows. This is especially useful when you are deep in your terminal running Claude Code and want a quick usage check without breaking your workflow.

Setting Up ClaudeUsageBar in 3 Minutes

Getting started is straightforward. The entire setup takes less than three minutes, and there are no dependencies, no Homebrew formulas, and no build steps.

  1. Download the installer. Grab the latest .dmg file from the GitHub releases page. The app is under 5MB.
  2. Drag to Applications. Open the DMG and drag ClaudeUsageBar into your Applications folder, just like any other macOS app.
  3. Get your session cookie. Go to claude.ai/settings/usage, open Developer Tools (Cmd+Option+I), navigate to the Network tab, refresh the page, click on the "usage" request, and copy the full Cookie value from the Request Headers. For a detailed walkthrough with screenshots, check our guide on how to check Claude usage limits.
  4. Paste the cookie into ClaudeUsageBar. Launch the app, click the menu bar icon, select "Set Session Cookie," and paste the cookie value. The app immediately fetches your usage data and starts displaying it.

That is it. The app starts at login by default, runs silently in the background, and keeps your usage visible at all times.

Claude Code Usage Limits: What You Should Know

Understanding Claude Code's rate limiting structure helps you get the most out of your subscription. Here is how the limits work.

Anthropic structures Claude usage around two limit windows: a shorter session window and a longer weekly window. The session window typically resets every few hours, giving you a burst of capacity. The weekly window is a broader cap on total usage over a seven-day rolling period.

The exact limits vary based on your plan tier and which model you are using. Opus-class requests consume more of your allowance than Sonnet-class requests. If you are on the Pro plan, your limits are higher than the Free tier but still finite. The key insight is that both windows matter. You might have session capacity available but be constrained by your weekly limit, or vice versa.

The developers who get the most out of Claude Code are the ones who treat their usage budget like any other finite resource. They plan high-leverage prompts, batch related questions, and pace themselves across the week.

ClaudeUsageBar surfaces both limit windows simultaneously so you always have the full picture. You can see at a glance whether you are constrained by your session or your weekly budget and plan accordingly. This kind of visibility turns usage management from a frustrating guessing game into a deliberate part of your development workflow.
